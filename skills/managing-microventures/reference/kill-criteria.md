# Kill criteria — Annie Duke

Source: **Annie Duke, *Quit: The Power of Knowing When to Walk Away* (2022)** — building on her
decision-science work in *Thinking in Bets*.

The core problem: humans are terrible at quitting on time. Sunk cost, escalation of commitment, and
the fear of "wasting" what we've already spent keep us in dead bets long past the point where
continuing is the real waste. Kill criteria fix this by deciding **in advance**, while we're still
cool-headed, what evidence would mean "stop".

## The ideas

- **States + dates, set in advance.** A kill trigger names a concrete *state* ("fewer than 50 weekly
  actives") **and** a *date* ("by 2026-06-30"). Set before you're emotionally invested. **Immutable
  after the fact** — you don't get to move the line once you can see where the ball landed.
- **The P(success) gut-check.** Periodically ask honestly: "knowing what I know now, what's the
  probability this works?" A number you'd be embarrassed to say out loud is a signal.
- **Monkeys & pedestals** (from Astro Teller / X). If the hard part is training a monkey to recite
  Shakespeare, don't start by building the pedestal. Test the **monkey** (the thing most likely to
  kill the project) first. Building the easy part feels like progress and proves nothing.
- **Sunk cost & escalation.** What you've already spent is gone whether you continue or not. It is
  never a reason to continue.
- **"Waste is what's ahead."** The waste isn't the time already spent — it's the time you'll spend
  *from here* on a bet you already have evidence against.
- **The quitting coach.** Name someone (or something) whose job is to hold you to the criteria when
  you're tempted to rationalize. In this skill, **that coach is the agent.**

## How the skill / lenses use it

- **Gate 1** writes ≥2 state+date kill triggers and an honest P% into `## Kill line`.
- **⏳ keeper-of-time** is the quitting coach made into a lens: it compares the live metric to the
  triggers **as written**, refuses goalpost-moving, and breaks toward KILL on the fence at a fired
  trigger.
- **Hard rule:** a fired trigger is **never reframed** into a reason to continue, and `/override`
  never erases it.
- **Monkeys & pedestals** drives "riskiest assumption first": the first experiment must test the
  monkey, and ⏳ checks that it did.
