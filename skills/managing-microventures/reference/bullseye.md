# Bullseye — traction channels

Source: **Gabriel Weinberg & Justin Mares, *Traction* (2015)** — the Bullseye framework for finding
the channel that will actually grow the venture.

## The 19 channels

Most founders default to one or two channels they're comfortable with and never test the rest.
Bullseye forces breadth. The 19 traction channels:

1. Viral marketing 2. Public relations (PR) 3. Unconventional PR (stunts) 4. Search engine
marketing (SEM) 5. Social & display ads 6. Offline ads 7. Search engine optimization (SEO)
8. Content marketing 9. Email marketing 10. Engineering as marketing (tools/widgets)
11. Targeting blogs 12. Business development 13. Sales 14. Affiliate programs 15. Existing
platforms (app stores, marketplaces) 16. Trade shows 17. Offline events 18. Speaking engagements
19. Community building.

## The Bullseye process

1. **Outer ring — brainstorm.** Think of at least one plausible way to use *every* channel. No
   dismissing channels from the armchair.
2. **Middle ring — pick 3.** Choose the 3 most promising to test now.
3. **Inner ring — run cheap parallel tests.** Run small, cheap, time-boxed experiments on those 3 in
   parallel. The question is: *which channel is showing traction at acceptable cost?*
4. **Focus.** Once one channel is clearly working, pour effort into it until it stops working, then
   re-run Bullseye.

## The 50% rule and the kill signal

- **The 50% rule:** spend roughly half your effort on the **product** and half on **traction**.
  Building in a vacuum with no distribution is a classic solo-founder failure.
- **"No channel works" is a kill signal.** If you've cycled Bullseye honestly — brainstormed all 19,
  tested several in cheap parallel rounds — and **no channel** reaches acceptable cost, that's strong
  evidence the bet is dead, not that you need a 20th idea.

## How the skill / lenses use it

- **`## Channels`** in `VENTURE.md` records the tested channels and each result.
- **🕳 demand-scout** treats "no channel works after honest cycling" as a kill-trigger mover and checks
  whether distribution, not just the product, is the bottleneck.
- **Gate 2** weighs channel results; **Gate 3** can pivot the **channel** element specifically.
