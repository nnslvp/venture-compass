# AARRR + One Metric That Matters

Sources: **Dave McClure, "AARRR! Startup Metrics for Pirates" (2007)** and **Alistair Croll & Benjamin
Yoskovitz, *Lean Analytics* (2013)** for One Metric That Matters and the vanity-metric warning.

## AARRR — the pirate funnel

Five stages a user moves through:

1. **Acquisition** — they find you.
2. **Activation** — first good experience ("aha").
3. **Retention** — they come back.
4. **Referral** — they bring others.
5. **Revenue** — they pay.

For a popularity / audience play (no money yet), use **AARRR minus Revenue** — the success metric is
sustained Retention and Referral (an audience that returns and spreads), not dollars.

## One Metric That Matters (OMTM)

At any one time, focus on **the single metric** that matters for the current stage and bet. It:
- forces a clear question and a clear answer,
- has a **target line** and a **kill line**, and
- changes as the venture moves stages (the activation metric early, retention later).

## Vanity-metric warning

A **vanity metric** goes up and to the right and makes you feel good but doesn't change a decision:
cumulative signups, total pageviews, raw downloads. Prefer metrics that are **actionable, comparable,
and ratio/cohort-based**: weekly actives, day-7 retention, activation rate, cost-per-engaged-user.
The test: *if this number doubled, would I do anything differently?* If not, it's vanity.

## 1000 true fans (direction, not guarantee)

**Kevin Kelly's "1000 True Fans":** a creator needs only ~1000 people who'll buy anything you make.
For a micro-venture it's a **direction** — depth of a small, committed audience can be the whole win —
**not a guarantee**: 1000 fans you can't actually reach or who won't commit is still a dead bet.

## How the skill / lenses use it

- **Gate 1** picks the OMTM and writes its target and kill lines; **🧰 metrics-engineer** makes that
  OMTM automatically measurable.
- **💼 business-pragmatist** judges the OMTM vs threshold and rejects vanity metrics.
- **🕳 demand-scout** counts non-monetary demand (retention/referral, 1000-true-fans) as real
  traction for popularity plays.
- **Gate 2** reads the OMTM **live** from the source, never "со слов".
