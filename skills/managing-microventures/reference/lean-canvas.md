# Lean Canvas + The Mom Test

Sources: **Ash Maurya, *Running Lean* (Lean Canvas, adapted from Osterwalder's Business Model
Canvas)** and **Rob Fitzpatrick, *The Mom Test* (2013)**. Underneath both: Eric Ries's *Lean
Startup* and Steve Blank's customer development — treat the plan as a stack of hypotheses to test,
not a spec to execute.

## Lean Canvas — 9 boxes as hypotheses

A one-page model. Each box is a **hypothesis**, not a fact:

1. **Problem** (top 1–3) 2. **Customer segments** 3. **Unique value proposition**
4. **Solution** 5. **Channels** 6. **Revenue streams** 7. **Cost structure**
8. **Key metrics** 9. **Unfair advantage**

Principles:
- **Love the problem, not the solution.** Founders fall for their solution and stop testing whether
  the problem is real or painful enough.
- **Riskiest-assumption-first.** Don't validate boxes in order — find the assumption whose failure
  kills everything and test that first (this is the monkey).
- **Versioned snapshots.** The canvas changes as you learn. Keep dated versions so a pivot is a
  visible, deliberate change of one box, not silent drift.

## The Mom Test — how to get real signal

Fitzpatrick's rule: ask questions even your mom couldn't lie to you on. You do this by talking about
**their life, not your idea**:

- **Talk about their life and past behavior**, not your idea and not hypotheticals. "Walk me through
  the last time you hit this" beats "would you use a tool that…".
- **Discard compliments, hypotheticals, and wishlists** — they're noise. "That's a great idea" and
  "I'd definitely buy that" are not data.
- **A real signal is a commitment**: money, time, reputation, a pre-order, a calendar slot, an
  introduction. Anything that costs them something.

## How the skill / lenses use it

- **Gate 0 & 1** build and version the canvas in `## Lean Canvas`; the riskiest box becomes the
  monkey.
- **🕳 demand-scout** applies The Mom Test to every demand claim: it looks for past behavior and
  commitments, and **discards** "no competitors", compliments, and "would you" hypotheticals.
- **Gate 3** pivots change exactly **one** canvas box (audience / problem / solution / channel) and
  bump the version.
